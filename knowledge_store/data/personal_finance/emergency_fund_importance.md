Title: Importance of an Emergency Fund
Category: personal_finance
Tags: emergency_fund, financial_security, liquidity
Risk Profile: all
Target Audience: beginner
Investor Horizon: short
Last Updated: 2026-03

Key Points:
- Emergency funds cover unexpected financial events.
- Experts recommend saving 3–6 months of living expenses.
- Funds should be kept in highly liquid accounts.
- Emergency savings prevent forced investment withdrawals.

Content:
An emergency fund is a financial safety net used to cover
unexpected expenses such as medical bills, job loss, or
urgent home repairs.

Financial planners commonly recommend maintaining savings
equivalent to three to six months of living expenses. These
funds should be stored in highly liquid accounts such as
high-yield savings accounts or money market funds.

Having an emergency fund allows investors to avoid selling
long-term investments during market downturns. This helps
preserve long-term financial plans while managing short-term
financial shocks.
