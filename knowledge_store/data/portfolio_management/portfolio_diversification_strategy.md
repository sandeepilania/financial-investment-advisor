Key Points:
- Diversification spreads investments across different assets.
- It reduces exposure to any single investment.
- Diversification can occur across industries and regions.
- Diversified portfolios often experience lower volatility.

Content:
Diversification is a strategy used to reduce investment risk by
spreading capital across different types of assets. Rather than
concentrating investments in a single company or sector, investors
allocate funds across multiple securities.

A diversified portfolio may include stocks from various industries,
government and corporate bonds, and international investments.
This approach reduces the likelihood that a single market event
will significantly impact the entire portfolio.

While diversification cannot eliminate risk entirely, it can help
investors achieve more stable long-term performance.
