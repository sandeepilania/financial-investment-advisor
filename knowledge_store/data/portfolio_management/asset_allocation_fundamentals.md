Key Points:

- Diversification reduces portfolio risk.
- Stocks, bonds, and cash play different roles.
- Allocation should reflect risk tolerance and time horizon.

Content:
Asset allocation refers to the strategy of distributing investments
across different asset classes such as equities, bonds, and cash.
The goal is to balance risk and return according to the investor's
financial objectives.

Stocks generally provide higher growth potential but also greater
volatility. Bonds typically offer lower returns but provide
stability and income through interest payments.

Cash equivalents offer liquidity and safety but usually produce
minimal returns. By combining these asset classes, investors can
create a portfolio that aligns with their financial goals and
risk tolerance.
