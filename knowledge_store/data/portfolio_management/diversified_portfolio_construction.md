Key Points:
- Diversification spreads investments across asset classes.
- It reduces the risk of concentrated losses.
- Global diversification may improve portfolio stability.
- Asset allocation plays a key role in diversification.

Content:
Diversified portfolio construction involves allocating
investments across multiple asset classes, sectors,
and geographic regions.

This strategy reduces reliance on the performance of
any single investment. For example, a diversified
portfolio might include equities, bonds, real estate,
and international securities.

Diversification can help smooth portfolio returns and
reduce the impact of individual market events.

While diversification does not eliminate risk, it
helps investors achieve more consistent long-term
investment performance.
