Key Points:

- They become more conservative as retirement approaches.
- These funds simplify long-term investing.
- Investors choose funds based on expected retirement year.

Content:
Target date funds are investment funds designed to
automatically adjust asset allocation as investors
approach retirement.

These funds typically begin with a higher allocation
to equities for growth. As the target retirement
date approaches, the portfolio gradually shifts
toward bonds and other lower-risk assets.

This automatic adjustment is known as a glide path.

Target date funds are commonly used in employer
retirement plans because they simplify investment
decisions for individual investors.
