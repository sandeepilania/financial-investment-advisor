Key Points:
- Rebalancing restores original portfolio allocation.
- Market movements can shift asset weights over time.
- Periodic rebalancing maintains risk levels.
- Many investors rebalance annually or semiannually.

Content:
Portfolio rebalancing is the process of adjusting investment
allocations to maintain a desired asset distribution.

Over time, market performance can cause certain assets
to grow faster than others. For example, a strong stock
market rally may cause equities to represent a larger
percentage of a portfolio than originally intended.

Rebalancing involves selling a portion of the outperforming
assets and reinvesting the proceeds into underweighted
assets. This restores the portfolio's target allocation.

Regular rebalancing helps investors maintain consistent
risk exposure and encourages disciplined investing.
