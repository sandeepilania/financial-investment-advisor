Content:
Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS) are U.S.
government bonds designed to protect investors from the
effects of inflation.

Unlike traditional bonds, the principal value of TIPS
adjusts with changes in the Consumer Price Index (CPI).
As inflation increases, the bond's principal rises,
resulting in larger interest payments.

At maturity, investors receive either the adjusted
principal or the original principal, whichever is greater.

TIPS are commonly used by conservative investors who
want to protect their purchasing power without taking
significant market risk.
