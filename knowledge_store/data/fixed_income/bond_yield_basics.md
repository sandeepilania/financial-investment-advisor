Key Points:
- Bond yield measures the return an investor receives.
- Yield depends on coupon payments and bond price.
- Bond prices and yields move in opposite directions.
- Yield is an important indicator of bond attractiveness.

Content:
Bond yield represents the return an investor earns from
holding a bond. The most basic form is the coupon yield,
which is based on the interest payments relative to the
bond's face value.

However, bond prices fluctuate in the market. When bond
prices rise, yields fall, and when prices fall, yields
increase.

Investors often analyze yield to determine whether a bond
offers adequate compensation for its risk.

Understanding bond yields helps investors compare different
fixed income securities and evaluate their role in a
portfolio.
