Content:
Government bonds are debt securities issued by national governments
to finance public spending. In the United States, these securities
are issued by the U.S. Treasury and include Treasury bills,
Treasury notes, and Treasury bonds.

Treasury securities are generally considered among the safest
investments because they are backed by the full faith and credit
of the U.S. government.

Investors purchase government bonds in exchange for periodic
interest payments and the return of principal at maturity.
Because of their relatively low risk, government bonds often
provide lower returns than equities.

Many investors include government bonds in their portfolios to
reduce overall risk and generate stable income.
