Key Points:

- It helps manage interest rate risk.
- Provides predictable income streams.
- Investors reinvest maturing bonds at current rates.

Content:
Bond laddering is an investment strategy that involves
purchasing bonds with different maturity dates.

For example, an investor might hold bonds maturing in
1, 3, 5, and 10 years. As shorter-term bonds mature,
the proceeds can be reinvested into new bonds at
current market interest rates.

This strategy helps investors manage interest rate risk
while maintaining a consistent income stream.

Bond laddering is commonly used by investors who rely on
fixed income investments for regular cash flow, such as
retirees.
