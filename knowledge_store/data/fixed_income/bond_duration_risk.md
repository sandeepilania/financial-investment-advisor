Key Points:
- Duration measures bond sensitivity to interest rates.
- Higher duration bonds experience larger price changes.
- Interest rate increases typically reduce bond prices.
- Duration helps investors manage fixed income risk.

Content:
Bond duration is a measure used to estimate how sensitive
a bond's price is to changes in interest rates. Bonds with
longer durations tend to experience larger price changes
when interest rates move.

For example, if interest rates increase, the value of
existing bonds with lower coupon rates may decline because
new bonds are issued with higher yields.

Duration helps investors understand the level of interest
rate risk within their fixed income investments.

Investors often balance short-duration and long-duration
bonds depending on their expectations for future interest
rate movements.
