Key Points:
- Corporate bonds are issued by companies to raise capital.
- They typically offer higher yields than government bonds.
- Credit risk varies depending on the issuer.
- Bond ratings help investors evaluate risk.

Content:
Corporate bonds are debt securities issued by companies to
finance business operations, acquisitions, or expansion.

When investors buy corporate bonds, they are effectively
lending money to the issuing company in exchange for
regular interest payments and repayment of the principal
at maturity.

Because companies carry varying levels of financial risk,
corporate bonds typically offer higher yields than
government bonds.

Credit rating agencies such as Moody's, S&P, and Fitch
evaluate corporate bond issuers and assign credit ratings
that help investors assess the likelihood of repayment.
