Key Points:
- Index funds track a specific market index.
- They provide broad diversification.
- Passive investing often results in lower fees.
- Long-term investors commonly use index funds.

Content:
Index funds are investment funds designed to replicate the
performance of a specific market index such as the S&P 500 or
the total stock market.

Rather than actively selecting stocks, index funds simply
purchase all the securities in the target index. This passive
approach reduces management costs and typically results in
lower expense ratios compared to actively managed funds.

Index investing has gained popularity because many studies
show that passive strategies often outperform actively managed
funds over long time horizons.

For long-term investors seeking diversified market exposure
with low costs, index funds are often considered a core
portfolio component.
