Content:
Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs) are pooled investment vehicles that hold
a basket of securities such as stocks or bonds. Unlike traditional
mutual funds, ETFs trade on stock exchanges throughout the day,
similar to individual stocks.

Many ETFs are designed to track the performance of a market index,
such as the S&P 500 or Nasdaq-100. Because they follow a passive
investment strategy, ETFs generally have lower management fees.

ETFs allow investors to achieve diversification quickly. Instead of
buying many individual stocks, investors can purchase a single ETF
that represents an entire market segment.

Due to their flexibility, liquidity, and relatively low costs, ETFs
have become one of the most popular investment products among both
individual and institutional investors.
