Category: behavioral_finance

Risk Profile: all
Target Audience: intermediate

Last Updated: 2026-03

Key Points:
- Overconfidence leads investors to overestimate their abilities.
- Excessive trading often reduces investment returns.
- Investors may underestimate risks.
- Diversification helps mitigate overconfidence effects.

Content:
Overconfidence bias occurs when investors believe
their knowledge or market predictions are more
accurate than they actually are.

This bias can lead to excessive trading, market
timing attempts, and concentration in a few
investments.

Research shows that investors who trade frequently
often achieve lower returns due to transaction
costs and poor timing decisions.

Maintaining diversified portfolios and long-term
investment strategies can help mitigate the
effects of overconfidence.
