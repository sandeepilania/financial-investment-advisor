Key Points:

- Social influence often drives investment trends.
- Independent analysis helps avoid herd-driven mistakes.

Content:
Herd behavior occurs when investors follow the actions
of the majority rather than making independent
investment decisions.

This phenomenon is common during speculative bubbles
when many investors buy assets simply because others
are doing the same.

Herd behavior can also lead to sharp market declines
when investors collectively panic and sell assets.

Successful long-term investors typically rely on
careful analysis and disciplined strategies rather
than crowd-driven decisions.
