Key Points:
- Investors feel losses more strongly than gains.
- Loss aversion can lead to poor investment decisions.
- Investors may hold losing assets too long.
- Rational portfolio management requires discipline.

Content:
Loss aversion is a behavioral bias where investors
experience the pain of losses more intensely than
the satisfaction of equivalent gains.

Because of this bias, investors may hesitate to sell
losing investments in the hope that prices will
recover. This can lead to portfolios becoming
concentrated in underperforming assets.

Loss aversion can also cause investors to sell
winning investments too early to "lock in gains."

Financial advisors help clients overcome loss
aversion by encouraging disciplined portfolio
management and long-term investment planning.
