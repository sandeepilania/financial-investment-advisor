Key Points:
- Investor sentiment often swings between fear and greed.
- Market bubbles may form during periods of excessive optimism.
- Panic selling can occur during market downturns.
- Long-term investors benefit from avoiding emotional reactions.

Content:
Financial markets are heavily influenced by investor
psychology. Two dominant emotions that drive market
behavior are fear and greed.

During strong market rallies, investors may become
overly optimistic and take excessive risks. This
behavior can lead to speculative bubbles.

Conversely, during market downturns, fear can drive
investors to sell assets at depressed prices.

Disciplined long-term investors attempt to avoid
reacting to these emotional cycles and instead
focus on maintaining a consistent investment
strategy.
