Key Points:

- Awareness of biases can improve investment outcomes.

Content:
Behavioral biases refer to psychological tendencies that
affect financial decision-making. These biases can cause
investors to deviate from rational investment strategies.

Common behavioral biases include overconfidence, loss
aversion, and herd behavior. These tendencies often lead
investors to buy during market booms and sell during
market downturns.

Financial advisors frequently help clients recognize
these biases and maintain disciplined investment
strategies.

Understanding behavioral finance can help investors
make better long-term decisions and avoid emotional
mistakes.
