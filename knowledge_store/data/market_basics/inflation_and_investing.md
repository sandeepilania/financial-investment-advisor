Title: Inflation and Its Impact on Investments
Category: market_basics
Tags: inflation, purchasing_power, economic_cycles
Risk Profile: all
Target Audience: beginner
Investor Horizon: long
Last Updated: 2026-03

Key Points:
- Inflation reduces the purchasing power of money.
- Investments must grow faster than inflation to preserve wealth.
- Stocks and real assets often provide better inflation protection.
- Fixed income investments may lose value during high inflation.

Content:
Inflation refers to the gradual increase in prices of goods and
services over time. As inflation rises, the purchasing power of
money declines.

For investors, inflation represents a major challenge because
cash savings may lose value in real terms. If investments fail
to generate returns greater than the inflation rate, an
investor's real wealth decreases.

Historically, equities have provided stronger protection against
inflation because companies can raise prices and maintain
profitability.

Understanding inflation helps investors design portfolios that
maintain purchasing power over long investment horizons.
