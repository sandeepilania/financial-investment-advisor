Key Points:
- Central banks influence interest rates through monetary policy.
- Rising interest rates can slow economic growth.
- Higher rates may reduce stock valuations.
- Bond prices typically fall when interest rates rise.

Content:
Interest rates represent the cost of borrowing money. Central
banks, such as the Federal Reserve, influence interest rates
to manage economic growth and inflation.

When interest rates rise, borrowing becomes more expensive
for businesses and consumers. This can slow economic activity
and reduce corporate profits.

Higher interest rates also impact financial markets. Bond
prices tend to decline because newly issued bonds offer
higher yields. Equity markets may also experience pressure
as investors shift toward safer fixed income investments.

Understanding interest rate cycles helps investors make
informed portfolio allocation decisions.
