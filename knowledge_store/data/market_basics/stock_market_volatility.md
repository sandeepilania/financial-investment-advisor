Key Points:

- Long-term investors typically benefit from staying invested.
- Emotional reactions can harm investment decisions.

Content:
Stock market volatility refers to the degree of variation in
the price of financial assets over time. Markets naturally
experience periods of rising and falling prices.

Short-term volatility can be caused by economic news,
interest rate changes, geopolitical events, or investor
sentiment.

Although volatility may create anxiety for investors,
historical data shows that markets tend to grow over the
long term. Investors who remain disciplined and avoid
panic selling during downturns often achieve better
long-term results.

Understanding volatility helps investors maintain a
long-term perspective and avoid emotional investment
decisions.
