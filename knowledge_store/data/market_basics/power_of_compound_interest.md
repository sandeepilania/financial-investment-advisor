Key Points:
- Compound interest allows earnings to generate additional earnings.
- Starting early dramatically increases investment growth.
- Consistent investing benefits from compounding.
- Time is one of the most powerful drivers of investment growth.

Content:
Compound interest occurs when investment earnings generate
additional earnings over time. Instead of earning returns only
on the original investment, investors earn returns on accumulated
gains as well.

This compounding effect becomes increasingly powerful over
long periods. For example, investments held for several decades
may grow exponentially compared to short-term investments.

Starting early allows investors to maximize the benefits of
compound growth. Even small contributions made consistently
over many years can lead to substantial wealth accumulation.
