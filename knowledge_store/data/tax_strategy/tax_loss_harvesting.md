Key Points:
- Tax loss harvesting involves selling investments at a loss.
- Losses can offset capital gains taxes.
- Investors may reinvest in similar assets.
- IRS wash sale rules must be followed.

Content:
Tax loss harvesting is a strategy used to reduce taxable income
by selling investments that have declined in value.

When investors realize losses, they can use them to offset
capital gains generated from other investments. This can
reduce the overall tax burden for the year.

After selling a losing investment, investors often reinvest
the proceeds into similar securities to maintain portfolio
exposure.

However, the IRS wash sale rule prevents investors from
repurchasing the same or substantially identical security
within 30 days of selling it.
