Key Points:
- Asset location refers to placing investments in the right accounts.
- Tax-efficient assets can be held in taxable accounts.
- Income-generating assets may be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts.
- Strategic asset location can improve after-tax returns.

Content:
Asset location is the strategy of placing different investments
in specific account types to maximize tax efficiency.

For example, tax-efficient investments such as index funds
may be held in taxable brokerage accounts because they
generate fewer taxable distributions.

Income-producing assets such as bonds may be better placed
in tax-advantaged accounts like IRAs where interest income
is shielded from annual taxation.

By combining tax-efficient investing with strategic asset
location, investors can improve long-term after-tax returns.
