Title: Roth IRA vs Traditional IRA
Category: tax_strategy

Last Updated: 2026-03

Key Points:
- Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
- Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions on contributions.
- Choice depends on current and expected tax rates.
- Both accounts offer tax-advantaged investment growth.

Content:
Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs) provide tax advantages
for long-term retirement savings.

Traditional IRAs allow investors to deduct contributions
from taxable income in the year they are made. However,
withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.

Roth IRAs work differently. Contributions are made using
after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals during
retirement are tax free.

Investors often choose between these accounts based on
their current tax bracket and expectations for future
income levels.
