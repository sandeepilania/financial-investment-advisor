Key Points:
- Capital gains tax applies when investments are sold for profit.
- Short-term gains are taxed at higher rates.
- Long-term gains receive favorable tax treatment.
- Holding investments longer may reduce taxes.

Content:
Capital gains tax is the tax applied to profits earned from selling
an investment such as stocks, bonds, or real estate.

Short-term capital gains apply to investments held for one year
or less and are typically taxed at ordinary income tax rates.
Long-term capital gains apply to investments held for more than
one year and often benefit from lower tax rates.

Because of this difference, many investors adopt long-term
investment strategies to minimize tax liabilities.

Understanding capital gains taxes helps investors make more
efficient decisions when buying and selling investments.
