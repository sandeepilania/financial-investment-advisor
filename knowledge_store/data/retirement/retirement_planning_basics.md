Key Points:
- Start saving early to benefit from compounding.
- Employer 401(k) matching contributions increase savings.
- IRAs provide additional tax advantages.
- Portfolio risk should decrease as retirement approaches.

Content:
Retirement planning is the process of building financial resources
to support living expenses after leaving the workforce. Because
retirement can last several decades, long-term investing strategies
are often required.

Employer-sponsored retirement plans such as 401(k)s are a common
starting point. Many employers match a portion of employee
contributions, effectively increasing retirement savings.

Investors can also contribute to Individual Retirement Accounts
(IRAs). Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth while Roth IRAs
allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

As retirement approaches, portfolios are typically adjusted to
reduce volatility. This often involves gradually shifting from
equities toward bonds and other income-generating assets.
