Content:
Dollar cost averaging is an investment strategy where an investor
regularly invests a fixed amount of money regardless of market
conditions.

For example, an investor may invest a set amount every month
into a mutual fund or ETF. When prices are low, the fixed
investment purchases more shares. When prices are high, fewer
shares are purchased.

This strategy helps reduce the risk of investing a large sum
of money at an unfavorable time. It also encourages disciplined
saving and long-term investment behavior.

Dollar cost averaging is commonly used in retirement accounts
such as 401(k) plans where contributions occur automatically
with each paycheck.
