Key Points:
- Certain assets perform better during inflation.
- Real assets such as commodities and real estate can hedge inflation.
- Equities may provide long-term inflation protection.
- Diversification helps manage inflation risk.

Content:
Inflation hedging refers to strategies used to protect
investment portfolios from the effects of rising prices.

Real assets such as commodities, infrastructure, and real
estate often increase in value when inflation rises. These
assets can provide a hedge against declining purchasing power.

Equities may also provide inflation protection because
companies can adjust prices for their goods and services.

Investors often diversify across asset classes to reduce
the impact of inflation on their overall portfolio.
