Key Points:
- Volatility measures the variability of investment returns.
- Higher volatility indicates greater uncertainty.
- Diversification can reduce portfolio volatility.
- Investors must balance volatility and return expectations.

Content:
Portfolio volatility refers to the degree of variation
in the value of investments over time.

Investments such as stocks often exhibit higher
volatility than bonds or cash equivalents.

While volatility can create short-term uncertainty,
it is also associated with higher potential returns.

Diversification across asset classes, industries,
and geographic regions can help reduce portfolio
volatility and create more stable investment
performance.
