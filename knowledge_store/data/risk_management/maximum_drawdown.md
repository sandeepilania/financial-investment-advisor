Key Points:
- Maximum drawdown measures the largest portfolio decline.
- It reflects worst-case historical loss.
- Investors use drawdown to evaluate downside risk.
- Strategies with lower drawdowns may be preferred.

Content:
Maximum drawdown represents the largest peak-to-trough
decline in the value of an investment portfolio over a
specific time period.

This metric helps investors understand the potential
downside risk associated with an investment strategy.

For example, if a portfolio falls from $100,000 to
$70,000 before recovering, the maximum drawdown is
30 percent.

Investors often consider drawdown when evaluating
portfolio resilience during market downturns.
