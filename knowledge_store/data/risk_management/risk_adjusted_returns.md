Key Points:
- Risk-adjusted returns measure performance relative to risk.
- Higher returns are not always better if risk is excessive.
- Metrics such as Sharpe Ratio evaluate risk efficiency.
- Investors aim to maximize returns per unit of risk.

Content:
Risk-adjusted returns evaluate investment performance
while accounting for the amount of risk taken to
achieve those returns.

Two portfolios may generate the same returns, but
the portfolio with lower volatility is generally
considered superior.

Financial analysts commonly use metrics such as the
Sharpe Ratio and Sortino Ratio to measure risk-adjusted
performance.

These metrics help investors determine whether an
investment strategy provides adequate compensation
for the risks involved.
