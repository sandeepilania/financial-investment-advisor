Content:
The Sharpe Ratio is a widely used metric for evaluating
risk-adjusted investment performance.

It measures the excess return of an investment relative
to a risk-free rate, divided by the investment's
standard deviation.

A higher Sharpe Ratio indicates that an investment
generates more return for each unit of risk taken.

Investors frequently use the Sharpe Ratio to compare
different portfolios or investment strategies.
