Key Points:
- Risk tolerance measures an investor's comfort with volatility.
- Age, financial stability, and goals influence risk tolerance.
- High-risk tolerance often leads to higher equity exposure.
- Conservative investors prioritize capital preservation.

Content:
Risk tolerance is the degree of variability in investment returns
that an investor is willing to accept. It is a key factor in
determining an appropriate investment strategy.

Investors with higher risk tolerance may choose portfolios
containing a larger proportion of equities because they are
comfortable with short-term fluctuations.

Conversely, investors with lower risk tolerance may prefer safer
investments such as bonds or treasury securities. These assets
typically offer lower returns but provide greater stability.

Understanding risk tolerance helps financial advisors design
investment strategies that investors can maintain even during
periods of market volatility.
