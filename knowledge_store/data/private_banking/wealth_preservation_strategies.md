Key Points:

- Diversification across asset classes helps reduce risk.
- Tax planning plays an important role in preserving wealth.
- Conservative allocations may increase as wealth grows.

Content:
Wealth preservation strategies are designed to protect accumulated
assets while maintaining moderate growth over time.

High-net-worth investors often prioritize capital protection and
income generation over aggressive growth. This can involve a
combination of diversified equities, fixed income investments,
real assets, and alternative investments.

Tax-efficient investing and estate planning are also critical
components of wealth preservation. These strategies ensure that
wealth is protected from unnecessary tax burdens and can be
transferred efficiently to future generations.

Private banking advisors typically tailor wealth preservation
strategies based on client risk tolerance, liquidity needs,
and long-term financial goals.
