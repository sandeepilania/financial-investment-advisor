Key Points:
- Trusts are legal structures used to manage and transfer assets.
- Revocable trusts offer flexibility during life.
- Irrevocable trusts may support tax and asset protection goals.
- Trust structures are commonly used in multi-generational planning.

Content:
A trust is a legal arrangement in which one party holds and manages
assets for the benefit of another party.

Trusts are often used in estate planning to control how and when
assets are distributed. A revocable trust can generally be amended
or revoked during the grantor's lifetime, while an irrevocable trust
is usually more difficult to change once established.

High-net-worth families may use trusts to support privacy,
continuity of asset management, charitable planning, or more
structured wealth transfer across generations.

Because trust strategies can be complex and highly dependent on
legal and tax considerations, advisors typically coordinate with
estate attorneys when discussing trust-based planning.
