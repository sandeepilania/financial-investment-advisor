Title: Managing Concentrated Stock Risk
Category: private_banking
Tags: concentrated_positions, portfolio_risk, diversification
Risk Profile: moderate
Target Audience: high_net_worth
Investor Horizon: medium
Last Updated: 2026-03

Key Points:
- Concentrated positions increase portfolio risk.
- Diversification helps reduce exposure to single assets.
- Gradual liquidation strategies may reduce tax impact.
- Hedging strategies may protect large positions.

Content:
Concentrated stock risk occurs when a large portion of an
investor's wealth is tied to a single company or security.

This situation often arises for company founders, executives,
or employees with significant stock-based compensation.

A sudden decline in the value of the company can significantly
impact the investor's net worth.

Private banking advisors typically recommend diversification
strategies, such as gradual sales, hedging with options, or
reinvesting proceeds into diversified portfolios.

These strategies help reduce concentration risk while managing
tax implications.
