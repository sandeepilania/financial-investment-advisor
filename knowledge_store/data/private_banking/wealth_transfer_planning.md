Key Points:
- Wealth transfer planning focuses on moving assets across generations.
- Lifetime gifting may reduce the size of a taxable estate.
- Planning should align with family goals and governance.
- Tax, trust, and philanthropic strategies are often interconnected.

Content:
Wealth transfer planning involves designing a strategy to pass
assets to heirs, family members, or charitable organizations in
an efficient and intentional manner.

For high-net-worth clients, this often includes reviewing gifting
strategies, beneficiary designations, trust arrangements, business
succession plans, and philanthropic objectives.

The goal is not only to reduce friction and potential tax exposure,
but also to ensure that wealth transfer reflects the family's values
and long-term intentions.

Private banking conversations around wealth transfer frequently
involve collaboration among the client, advisor, attorney, and tax
specialist.
