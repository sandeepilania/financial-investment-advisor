Key Points:
- Estate planning prepares for the transfer of wealth after death.
- Common tools include wills, trusts, and beneficiary designations.
- Proper planning can reduce family conflict and administrative delays.
- Estate planning often works alongside tax and wealth transfer strategies.

Content:
Estate planning is the process of organizing how a person's assets
will be managed and distributed during incapacity or after death.

A comprehensive estate plan may include a will, powers of attorney,
healthcare directives, trusts, and updated beneficiary designations
for retirement accounts and insurance policies.

For high-net-worth families, estate planning often extends beyond
basic document preparation. It can involve succession planning,
multi-generational wealth transfer, charitable giving, and
coordination with tax professionals.

Private banking advisors typically help clients think through
estate goals and coordinate with attorneys and tax advisors to
support an overall wealth strategy.
